Press release · 10 August 2027

Nairobi. On election day, PeerPulse processed 47,212 signed polling-station tallies from 38,847 unique citizen devices across 290 constituencies — a parallel count of the Kenyan general election, signed by the citizens who witnessed it.

The network ran without a central server, without state cooperation, and without any single entity able to alter, suppress, or fake a result.

The aggregate variance between the citizen-signed parallel count and the official IEBC result is published in real time at peerpulse.app/elections/ke-2027.
